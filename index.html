Works in Progress

Estimating Geometry and Pose for Scene Reconstruction using Diffusion priors

We combine diffusion-based priors with geometric optimization to jointly estimate consistent geometry and motion of a scene. Our optimization performs gradient descent minimization of a simple least-squares objective by comparing the optical flow induced by affine-invariant depth and camera pose estimates against correspondences obtained via an off-the-shelf optical flow method. We adapt and fine-tune the pre-trained Stable Diffusion 3.5 model to jointly estimate affine-invariant depth and optical flow from a pair of RGB images. Our method unifies geometry and motion estimation with diffusion-based priors, enabling the reconstruction of 3D scenes from multiple views.

Scene Understanding and Perception using Multi-view Diffusion Priors

We propose a novel method for 3D scene understanding and perception that leverages multi-view diffusion priors to jointly estimate geometry and SVBRDF of indoor scenes. This is achieved by adapting the pre-trained Stable Video Diffusion model by conditioning it on multiple views of a scene, and training it to output depth and normal maps, as well as the spatially varying BRDF of a scene. The proposed model provides a complete 3D model of the scene enabling various downstream tasks such as relighting, novel view synthesis, and 3D reconstruction.
